# Hospital Quality Matching Platform | Creative Direction for Figma Make AI

---

## Core Principle

**Design with quiet confidence.** This is a tool that helps people make high-stakes decisions. The interface should feel controlled, calm, and honest—not flashy or sales-y. Patients come here anxious. Your job is to make them feel grounded.

---

## Visual Language

### Color Palette (Mood, not Specs)

Primary accent: **Teal/Cyan** (health, modern, trustworthy—like FEC's vitality but clinical)  
Secondary: **Deep navy** (authority, stability, medical credibility)  
Neutral foundation: **Off-white, soft grays** (breathing room, not sterile white)  
Status indicators: **Green for good outcomes, orange for caution, subtle red only for important warnings** (clear at a glance, no alarm fatigue)

The palette should feel **warm professional**, not cold tech or corporate. Think hospital rebrand energy, not banking app.

---

## Typography & Hierarchy

**Headlines**: Bold, generous line height, conversational scale (not tiny, not overdone). Should feel like someone who knows their stuff speaking directly to you.

**Body text**: Readable, 16px+, generous letter spacing. People are stressed. Make reading effortless.

**Data labels**: Clear hierarchy. Important metrics (readmission rate, volume) should visually "breathe" more than secondary info.

**Tone in labels**: Clinical but human. "Readmission Rate" not "Hospital Readmission Metric Score." No jargon unless unavoidable. If unavoidable, explain.

---

## Component Style

### Cards and Containers

**No hard corners.** Soft border radius (12–16px). This softens the clinical feel while keeping it modern.

**Subtle shadows and depth.** A hospital card should feel like it's *standing up* on the page, solid and real, not floating. Use shadows sparingly—depth through intention, not decoration.

**Ranked hospital cards**: Large, scannable. Rank number prominent (visual weight). Score visible but not preachy. Metrics below in a clean grid or list.

### Comparison Views

When comparing hospitals, **side-by-side should be the default.** Make differences obvious through color and typography weight, not just numbers. A patient should see "this one is better" in 2 seconds.

### Input Fields

**Generous padding, clear focus states.** When a patient selects their condition or enters hospitals, they need to feel the system is *listening*. Smooth state transitions (not instant—maybe 200ms), visual feedback that's immediate.

**Dropdown for condition**: Should feel like browsing, not picking. Clear icons or subtle illustrations next to each condition (visual scanning helps anxious people).

---

## Motion & Transitions

**Speed and restraint.** Transitions should feel *decisive*, not slow and ponderous.

- **Page reveals** (results appearing): 300–400ms. Feels snappy but not jarring. Like a confident professional pulling out a report.
- **Card hover states**: 150ms. Quick response = reassuring.
- **Explanation text reveals** (Claude-generated text): Subtle fade-in or gentle slide, 200ms. Not dramatic, just graceful entry.
- **Loading states**: Minimal animation (maybe a subtle pulse or gradient shift). Avoid spinners—they feel like waiting. Instead: "Analyzing your options..." + a calm, understated visual.

**No bouncy animations.** No springs or elasticity. This isn't a game. It's healthcare.

**Transitions between steps** (condition → hospitals → results): Should feel like forward momentum. Not page reloads—more like unfolding, revealing the next logical piece.

---

## Trust & Credibility Cues

### Data Visualization

**Metrics should be scannable but humble.** Show readmission rate clearly, but don't oversell. A simple, clean bar or number. Show comparison to national benchmark as a subtle reference line or text note, not a flashy graphic.

**Color code outcomes:** Green when above benchmark, neutral when at benchmark, orange when below. No shame—just honesty. This builds trust.

### Explanations

**Claude-generated reasoning** should appear in a distinct but not separated container. Maybe a slightly different background or a left border accent. Patients need to know this is AI-generated reasoning, not gospel. Light touch—don't scream "AI," just subtly distinguish it.

### Source Attribution

**CMS data** should be credited somewhere visible but not intrusive. Maybe a small footer on results or a "Data from CMS Hospital Compare" note. Transparency builds confidence.

---

## Layout & Information Density

**Generous whitespace.** This isn't dense analytics dashboard. It's a decision support tool. Let information *breathe*.

**Mobile-first thinking** (even if desktop is primary). Vertical stacking, clear touch targets, readable on small screens. Healthcare users are on phones during consultations.

**Progressive disclosure.** Show the ranked list first. Metrics and explanations below. Don't overwhelm at first glance.

---

## Voice in Microcopy

- **Error states**: Not "Invalid input." Try "We couldn't find that hospital. Check the spelling or browse the list."
- **Empty states**: Not "No results." Try "No hospitals match yet. Try adding one from your insurance list."
- **Success**: "Got it. Analyzing these hospitals for [Condition]..."

Warm, clear, human. Not robotic.

---

## Overall Aesthetic Balance

**Think**: Modern medical (not sterile clinical) meets approachable technology.

Reference Datacor for: clean navigation, professional spacing, trustworthy color restraint.  
Reference FEC for: bold typography choices, confident color accents, personal warmth.

**The result**: A platform that feels both **credible** (you can trust the data) and **compassionate** (we understand you're anxious).

---

## Interaction Philosophy

Users coming to this tool are making healthcare decisions. Every interaction should:
1. **Reduce anxiety** (clear, calm, no surprises)
2. **Build confidence** (honest data, good explanations)
3. **Enable speed** (fast transitions, clear next steps)
4. **Respect intelligence** (no hand-holding, just clarity)

The platform should disappear. Patients should focus on the information, not the design.

---

## Key Principles for Make AI

1. **Controlled creativity**: Modern and fresh, but not trendy. This tool will be used in 5 years.
2. **Warm authority**: Trustworthy and professional, but never cold or corporate.
3. **Motion with purpose**: Every transition should feel intentional, not decorative.
4. **Accessibility as foundation**: Clear contrast, readable type, logical flow. Not accessible as an afterthought.
5. **Human-centered**: Every interaction designed for someone under stress making a big decision.

---

## What to Emphasize to Make AI

"Design for someone who is anxious but intelligent. They need clarity and speed, not decoration. Make them feel heard and informed, not patronized. Use color and motion to guide confidence, not to impress."
